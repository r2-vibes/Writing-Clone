# Style Guide (Template)

> This file is generated when you first share your writing samples with the voice editor.
> It captures your unique writing voice, patterns, and anti-patterns.

## How to Build Your Style Guide

1. Share 3-5 pieces of your best published writing (articles, essays, newsletters, blog posts)
2. The agent will analyze your voice and generate this file
3. Review it and correct anything that feels off
4. The guide improves with every editorial pass

## What Gets Captured

- **Voice DNA:** Your core identity as a writer, tone, emotional register
- **Signature moves:** Rhetorical techniques you use repeatedly
- **Sentence-level craft:** Length patterns, vocabulary, connective tissue
- **Anti-patterns:** Things you never do (these are critical for voice fidelity)
- **Paired examples:** Good (your voice) vs. wrong (generic AI) for every pattern
- **Thematic vocabulary:** Words you gravitate toward and words you avoid
- **Revision checklist:** Final gate before any piece ships as "your voice"
- **Correction history:** Every correction you make, logged permanently

## Example Anti-Patterns Table

| Pattern | Why It's Wrong | Fix |
|---------|---------------|-----|
| Em dashes everywhere | Common AI tell | Use commas, semicolons, periods |
| "Great question!" | Performative filler | Just answer |
| Generic summary closings | "In conclusion, X is important" | End with specificity or punch |
| Hedging core claims | "X might potentially help..." | State it with confidence |
| Passive voice (habitual) | "It was decided that..." | Active: "The regime decided..." |
